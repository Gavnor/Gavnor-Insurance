Understanding Life Insurance

Life insurance is a contract between you and an insurance provider. In exchange for premium payments, the insurer provides a lump-sum payment to beneficiaries upon the policyholder’s death.

Types of Life Insurance

  • Term Life Insurance: Covers you for a set period. Ideal for temporary coverage needs.
  • Whole Life Insurance: Lifetime coverage with a cash value component.
  • Universal Life Insurance: Flexible policy with investment savings element.

Our Life Insurance Products

Dhamana Life Insurance Savings Plan

Dhamana is a life assurance product designed to offer you a platform to save while enjoying a guaranteed investment and life insurance. It guarantees an investment rate of return of 5% for continuing policies. Once the policy has been in force for at least 3 years and is paid up to date, you can apply for a policy loan. Dhamana is affordable with a minimum premium of Kes 1000 per month with no restrictions on the maximum. It offers flexibility in premium payment; premium holidays are allowed when income levels fluctuate, and extra premium payments are also allowed. In the unfortunate event of the policyholder’s death, Dhamana will pay out a death benefit to the beneficiaries. Learn more

Akiba Savings Plan

The Akiba Savings Plan helps you build a risk-free savings fund and offers financial security to the family in the event of an unfortunate incident. It offers higher returns compared to other average insurance savings products. The plan is flexible, allowing you the option for lower policy terms between 5 and 12 years and is affordable. It allows for a higher entry age of up to 65 compared to most endowment products which are normally set at 55 years. Akiba pays a lump sum at maturity. Learn more

Imarika Investment Plan

The Imarika Investment Plan offers superior returns and insurance. At the end of your policy, you will receive the maximum of the fund value or the savings premiums earning the minimum guaranteed return of 5%. The policy comes with an inbuilt funeral cover of 100,000 payable to the beneficiary upon the death of a policyholder within 48 hours, subject to a policy value of 200,000 and above. If you need money in an emergency, you can withdraw your funds at any time during the plan. However, if you withdraw in Year 1, there’s a 5% charge, and in Year 2, a 3% charge. Learn more

Understanding Life Insurance

Life insurance is a contract between an individual and an insurance company, where the insurer promises to pay a designated beneficiary a sum of money upon the death of the insured person. This financial support can cover funeral expenses, debts, and provide for the family's future needs.

Benefits of Life Insurance
